Missing Scores?

Missing scores for the current year? There could be several reasons for this.

  • The information on your AP answer sheet is different from the information on your College Board account.

    Differences in name, date of birth, sex, and other information can prevent us from connecting your scores to your account. To resolve this issue, please contact customer service to have them connect your College Board account to your AP scores. You can use the online Contact Us form, email us at apstudents@info.collegeboard.org, or call 888-225-5427 (toll free) or 212-632-1780.

  • You tested late.

    If you took an exam between May 22 and May 24, 2019, your scores might not be available until late July or early August. You will receive an email when your scores are available. If you do not receive your scores by Sept. 1, please contact customer service.

  • There was a special circumstance on test day.

    Some scores take longer to process due to special circumstances such as late arrival of exam materials. You will receive an email when your score is available. If you do not receive your scores by Sept. 1, please contact customer service.

Missing scores for previous years?

  • The most recent AP Exam you took was more than four years ago.

    Your scores are archived and not available online. Visit Score Reporting Services for the Archived AP Scores Request Form to request your scores be sent to a college, university, or scholarship program.

If you need further help, email AP Services at apstudents@info.collegeboard.org or call 888-225-5427 (toll free) or 212-632-1780.
